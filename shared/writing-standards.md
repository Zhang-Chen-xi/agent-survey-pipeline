# Academic Writing Standards

> Cross-cutting protocol for academic writing conventions, applicable across all writing modules.

## General Standards

### Language
- Use precise, formal language throughout
- Avoid colloquialisms, contractions, and slang
- Write in third person for literature review; first person plural ("we") for your own contributions
- Use discipline-specific terminology correctly and consistently

### Argumentation
- Make claims supported by evidence (citations or data)
- Acknowledge counterarguments and alternative explanations
- Distinguish between established facts and interpretations
- Note study limitations honestly and specifically

### Structure
- Clear topic sentences for every paragraph
- Logical flow between paragraphs (use explicit transitions)
- Parallel structure in lists and comparisons
- Each section should have a clear purpose stated in its opening

### Spacing and Layout
- Line spacing: 1.5x (360 twips) for body, 1.0x for references
- First-line indent: 2 characters (480 twips) for body paragraphs
- Paragraph spacing: 0.5 lines before/after headings
- Page size: A4 (210mm × 297mm)
- Margins: Top/Bottom 2.54cm, Left/Right 3.17cm

### Numbering
- Sections: 1, 1.1, 1.1.1 (Arabic numerals)
- Figures: 图1, 图2... (Chinese) or Fig. 1, Fig. 2... (English)
- Tables: 表1, 表2... (Chinese) or Table 1, Table 2... (English)
- Equations: (1), (2), (3)... right-aligned
- References: [1], [2], [3]... as superscript in text (GB/T 7714) or inline (APA)

## English Academic Writing Conventions

### Common Improvements
| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| "it can be seen that" | (remove, state directly) |
| "in order to" | "to" |
| "due to the fact that" | "because" |
| "a large number of" | "many" / "numerous" |
| "has the ability to" | "can" |
| "in the area of" | "in" |
| "at this point in time" | "currently" / "now" |

### Transition Words (Rotate to Avoid Repetition)
- Addition: furthermore, moreover, additionally, in addition, notably
- Contrast: however, in contrast, conversely, on the other hand, nevertheless
- Cause: consequently, therefore, as a result, thus, accordingly
- Example: for instance, specifically, in particular, to illustrate
- Summary: in summary, to summarize, overall, taken together

## Citation Placement Rules

1. **Cite after the claim, not after the author name** (preferred in most styles):
   - ✅ "This approach achieves state-of-the-art results [15]."
   - ✅ "Zhang et al. [15] demonstrated that..."

2. **Multiple citations**: Order chronologically or by relevance, not alphabetically:
   - ✅ "Several studies [3,7,12] have confirmed..." (chronological)
   - ❌ "Several studies [3,12,7] have confirmed..." (random)

3. **Citation density**: At least 1-2 citations per substantive claim in the literature review. Fewer in methodology and results sections.

## Figure and Table Standards

### Figures
- Resolution: 300 DPI minimum for raster images, vector preferred for charts
- Font size in figures: ≥8pt after scaling
- Caption below figure, numbered sequentially
- Reference every figure in text: "As shown in Fig. 1..."

### Tables
- Three-line table format (学术三线表) for Chinese papers
- Caption above table, numbered sequentially
- Reference every table in text: "Table 1 shows..."
- Use horizontal rules only (no vertical lines in academic tables)
